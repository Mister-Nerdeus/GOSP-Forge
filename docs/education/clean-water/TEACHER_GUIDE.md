# Clean Water Teacher Guide

This guide is free for public-school use.

## Use Boundary

The Clean Water foundation example is a supervised classroom activity for discussing system tradeoffs, assumptions, flow, power, cost, confidence, and safety boundaries. It is not potable-water certification, professional engineering approval, or real-world deployment approval.

## Teacher Setup

- Review the manifest, modules, product bindings, simulation output, estimate output, and validation warnings before class.
- Use clean demonstration materials only.
- Do not ask students to drink, taste, or certify any water.
- Keep electrical and water demonstrations supervised and low-voltage.

## Discussion Prompts

- Which inputs are measured, assumed, or defaulted?
- Which warnings reduce confidence?
- Which design choices affect downstream cost or runtime?
- What review would be required before any real-world use outside the classroom?
