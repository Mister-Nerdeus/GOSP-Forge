## Risk register

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---:|---|
| Scope explosion | High | one vertical slice first |
| Overbuilding 3D/CAD | High | asset manifests/previews first |
| Sponsor capture | High | sponsor independence charter |
| Pay-to-win perception | High | scoring neutrality rules |
| Unsafe modules | High | safety profiles and moderation |
| Student privacy | High | teacher-managed, privacy-by-default |
| AI hallucinated specs | High | field confidence and human review |
| Manufacturer bias | High | open product catalog and disclosures |
| IP/plagiarism disputes | High | attribution, licensing, takedown policy |
| Compute cost overrun | High | quotas, caching, sponsored pools |
| Cloud/AI lock-in | Medium | portable architecture |
| Engineer distrust | Medium | assumptions, units, confidence |
| Teacher complexity | Medium | education mode and guides |
| Gamer frustration | Medium | Dream Mode and quick sim |
| Bad data quality | High | confidence levels and provenance |
| Legal/financial complexity | Medium | phased governance path |
| Module spam | Medium | trust levels and moderation |
| Malicious uploads | High | file scanning and sandboxing |
| Fake product specs | High | verification statuses |
| Overclaiming estimates | High | estimate class and disclaimers |

## Compute risk

Gamers and public users may consume significant compute.

Mitigation:

- local-first simulation
- cached deterministic runs
- rate limits
- contribution credits
- sponsored compute pools
- education quotas
- queueing
- professional paid heavy compute

## Safety risk

Physical modules can affect water, electricity, structures, machinery, and health.

Mitigation:

- safety categories
- review requirements
- restricted modules
- disclaimers
- expert review for high-risk domains

## Financial risk

The project may depend on free credits.

Mitigation:

- in-kind ledger
- expiration tracking
- replacement plan
- cost dashboards
- avoid lock-in

## Governance risk

Sponsors may try to influence product or scores.

Mitigation:

- no sponsor majority
- public scoring rules
- disclosure
- conflict policy
- independent technical steering

## Final risk doctrine

> A useful simulator must be ambitious but honest. GOSP Forge should advance quickly, but every output must disclose assumptions, confidence, safety status, and provenance.
