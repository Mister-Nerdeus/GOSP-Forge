# Manufacturer Spec Program

## Purpose

Manufacturers can help make GOSP Forge more realistic by submitting product specifications, datasheets, manuals, safety sheets, CAD references, warranty data, and product availability information.

The goal is to make real products simulation-ready while preserving neutrality.

## Core policy

> Manufacturers may pay to improve data quality and verification. They may not pay to improve scores, suppress competitors, or control scoring rules.

## Why manufacturers should participate

Manufacturers get:

- simulation-ready product listings
- verified product badges
- educational exposure
- improved discoverability
- student-friendly spec explanations
- product compatibility with module packs
- public-good STEM participation
- sponsor recognition if they fund education

## Why GOSP Forge benefits

GOSP Forge gains:

- better product data
- more realistic simulations
- funding to keep schools free
- better STEM examples
- manufacturer accountability
- real-world specs for pricing and engineering comparison

## Product data levels

### Community-submitted

- free
- unverified
- public datasheet-based
- confidence low/medium

### Manufacturer-verified

- paid/sponsored
- manufacturer confirms specs
- confidence medium/high

### Simulation-ready binding

- paid/higher tier
- specs mapped to simulation engines
- compatibility rules added
- confidence medium/high

### Expert-reviewed

- professional/domain reviewer checks mapping
- highest confidence short of field validation

## Submission workflow

```text
1. Manufacturer account
2. Product submission
3. Datasheet/manual upload
4. AI extraction
5. Unit normalization
6. Missing-field report
7. Manufacturer review
8. Simulation mapping
9. Optional expert review
10. Publication
11. Version updates
```

## AI import pipeline

AI may extract:

- model number
- product category
- dimensions
- weight
- capacity
- voltage/current
- efficiency
- operating limits
- cost range
- warranty
- lifecycle
- safety limits
- installation requirements
- maintenance interval
- compatibility fields

AI must expose:

- extracted fields
- source references
- confidence per field
- missing data
- assumed values
- contradictions
- human review status

## ProductBinding contract

Product bindings are the core representation of manufactured products.

Important fields:

- manufacturer
- product name
- model number
- specs
- datasheets
- geometry
- cost
- availability
- warranty
- lifecycle
- compatibility
- provenance
- sponsorship disclosure

## Spec meaning

Every important spec should explain:

- what the number means
- what it affects
- where it is used in simulation
- what happens if it changes

Example:

```text
Battery usable capacity affects outage runtime, backup score, replacement planning, and cost.
```

## Pricing

Suggested early manufacturer program:

| Tier | Suggested Range | Includes |
|---|---:|---|
| Community listing | Free | unverified public listing |
| Basic verified SKU | $99–$250/SKU/year | verified manufacturer data |
| Simulation-ready binding | $500–$1,500/SKU/year | AI extraction + mapping |
| Product family pack | $2,500–$10,000/year | multiple SKUs |
| Education module sponsor | $10,000–$100,000 | public STEM content |
| Challenge sponsor | $25,000–$250,000 | public challenge support |

Prices are placeholders and should be adjusted after sponsor discovery.

## Disclosure labels

Product pages should show:

- Community Submitted
- Manufacturer Verified
- Expert Reviewed
- Sponsor Supported

Never use paid labels like:

- Preferred
- Best
- Official Winner

unless an unbiased score or challenge result supports it.

## Pay-to-win prohibition

Manufacturers cannot:

- buy better scores
- buy ranking advantage
- hide competitors
- block community-submitted alternatives
- privately change scoring
- control challenge rules
- require exclusive integration

## Public school policy

Manufacturer fees help keep public school teacher and student access free.

Public message:

> Commercial product data helps fund the educational commons.

## Sponsor opportunities

Manufacturers can sponsor:

- product family packs
- classroom kits
- STEM challenges
- hardware donations
- module pack development
- teacher guides
- fabrication demos

## Data maintenance

Product data must support:

- versioning
- expiration/reverification
- discontinued products
- replacement models
- updated specs
- price changes
- regional availability

## Legal and safety

Product submissions should include:

- datasheet permissions
- warranty disclaimers
- safety limits
- installation requirements
- usage restrictions
- no guarantee of system suitability unless reviewed

## Final principle

> Verified specs improve data confidence, not product favoritism.
